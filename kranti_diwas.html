Honoring India's struggle for Independence

Find Out For Yourself

As the sun rises on the 9th of August, the corridors of Delhi Public School (DPS) Varanasi echo with reverence and patriotism. On this auspicious day, we gather to commemorate Kranti Din, a significant milestone in India's struggle for independence. This day holds immense historical and emotional significance, and DPS Varanasi takes great pride in paying homage to the brave souls who paved the way for our nation's freedom

Kranti Din, also known as the Quit India Movement Day, marks the beginning of a powerful and determined movement against British colonial rule in 1942. This day serves as a reminder of the unyielding spirit of our forefathers who stood united against oppression, demanding the immediate withdrawal of British forces from our motherland. At DPS Varanasi, Kranti Din is not just a date on the calendar; it is a day of remembrance, reflection, and rekindling the flame of patriotism in the hearts of our students.

Kranti Din is not merely a historical event; it is a testament to the indomitable spirit and unwavering determination of the Indian people. Delhi Public School Varanasi's commemoration of this day serves as a bridge between the past and the present, reminding us of the sacrifices that were made for the freedoms we enjoy today. As we honor the heroes of our nation's past, we also pledge to uphold the values of unity, diversity, and freedom that they fought so hard to achieve. Join us in saluting the spirit of Kranti Din and carrying forward the legacy of our freedom fighters.
